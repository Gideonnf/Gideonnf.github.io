Kajaani University of Applied Sciences (June 2019 - August 2019)

During Internship, I went overseas to Finland's Kajaani University of Applied Sciences for three months. Over there, I had to work within a group consisting of other students from multiple countries. I was required to create a commercial game in 7 weeks during their Summer Game Course and either publish it on Itch.io, Steam or any other notable platforms.

Working in this new environment taught me a lot of new skills. I had picked up Unreal Engine during my time at this internship, as my team chose to create our game in Unreal Engine. I also had to learn to communicate a lot more. Working in a team of foreigners, we had a lot of commmunication issues. There were three nationalities in my team, Thai, Finnish and Singaporean. This brought a lot of communication issues as some of my team members couldn't converse in english that well. It was challenging at the start, we made a lot of conflicts in our code when we were trying to push due to a communication break down between the programmers. I had to learn how to communicate with them better, either expressing myself in simpler terms for them to understand, speaking slower or finding other means to communicate efficiently such as using Discord for urgent questions. Patiences was also something else that I had greatly improved in. Due to the langauge barrier, communication can be a bit slow when conversing in person thus I had to learn to be a bit more patient when talking to my team mates and repeating my words repeatedly.

Final Year Project 1 (February 2019 - May 2019)
Final Year Project 2 (October 2019 - February 2020)

In my Final Year Project 1, I had the opportunity to work with MOE to develope an undisclosed Game for them. It was quite a new experience for me, being my first client project that I worked on. For my Final Year Project 2, I worked on a VR Game built on the Oculus Quest for my school's Open House 2020.

These projects were the longest I had to work in, both being about 3 months or more. For the MOE Project, my team was the first team to take over the project that was meant to stretch over a period of time. Due to this, I had to take a lot more consideration into documentation and writing easily readable code as a few other teams will be taking over my work to continue. Communication in these projects were also very important as they are quite long with a decently sized team.

Working on the Oculus Quest for my second Final Year Project was also a new experience for me. Being the first team to work on the Oculus Quest, there were a lot of issues that I had to find ways around. The biggest issue being the lack of a debugger when I first started using it. There was no way to link the Oculus Quest with Unity during the early to middle stages of the game development. Thus, I had to create my own debugging tools that I can use to help make the debugging process a little bit less painful.
